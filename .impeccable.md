## Design Context

### Users
Casual fun-seekers and aesthetic-driven Gen Z visitors. They arrive curious, half-skeptical, half-hopeful. They will screenshot what they see and share it. They expect a quick, beautiful experience that respects their time — but rewards lingering. Their job-to-be-done is twofold: get a personal palm reading from a photo, and walk away with something share-worthy.

### Brand Personality
Three-word voice: **etched, dreamlike, contemplative**.
- Treats palmistry as celestial cartography, not parlor trick.
- Speaks like an annotated 17th-century manuscript: precise, poetic, a touch antique.
- Never winks at the user, never apologizes for the mysticism. Plays it straight, which is what makes it feel cool.
- Emotional goal: a hush. The kind of attention you give to looking at stars or opening an old book.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Old illustrated zodiac charts meet surreal cosmic dreamscape.**

Visual language draws from:
- Renaissance astronomical diagrams (Cellarius's Harmonia Macrocosmica)
- Hand-etched copperplate constellation maps
- Antique anatomical illustrations of the hand
- Color-field abstraction (Turrell-adjacent slow gradients) for atmosphere

Motion is slow, floaty, ambient. Lines draw themselves the way constellations are traced. Nothing snaps. Nothing bounces.

**Theme**: dual-mode with an intentional toggle.
- **Light**: warm parchment cream surfaces, deep ink text, gold-leaf accent. Reads as archival manuscript.
- **Dark**: deep midnight indigo (NOT black), parchment-toned text, same gold-leaf accent. Reads as night sky.
- The same celestial accent threads both modes for cohesion.

**Anti-references** — explicitly NOT:
- Generic AI-startup look (purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, neon glows, Inter)
- Crypto/web3 dark-mode neon
- Cheesy fortune-teller stock kit (crystal balls, neon Open signs, sparkles emoji)

### Design Principles
1. **Manuscript over interface.** Pages should feel like spreads in a book of celestial diagrams, not screens in an app. Generous margins. Marginalia. Numbered figures.
2. **Lines are sacred.** The palm's lines, the constellation lines, the etched borders — line work is the dominant visual motif. SVG strokes, not filled shapes.
3. **One accent, used rarely.** A single gold-leaf hue threads through both themes. It marks the reading itself, never decoration.
4. **Atmosphere over animation.** Slow, ambient motion (drifting starfields, lines drawing themselves) over snappy micro-interactions. Don't entertain — enchant.
5. **Reading as chapters.** The palm reading is presented as numbered chapters of a manuscript (Heart Line, Head Line, Life Line, Fate Line, Mounts), each its own beautiful page-turn moment.

### Typography Direction
- **Display**: IM Fell English — digitized 17th-century type with woodcut character. Fits the manuscript vibe without resorting to Fraunces/Playfair/Cormorant.
- **Body**: EB Garamond — historical, warm, readable in long passages.
- (Both available on Google Fonts; pairs avoid all reflex defaults.)

### The Unforgettable Moment
When the user uploads a photo of their palm, the image fades into a hand-etched line-drawing of itself. The principal lines (heart, head, life, fate) trace themselves across the photo with slow SVG stroke-draw animations, each labeled with a numbered marginalia note in the manuscript style — turning the user's own hand into a celestial chart.
